Pork Adobo

Description

Pork Adobo is a classic Filipino dish where chunks of pork—usually pork belly or shoulder—are braised in a mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns. The result is tender, flavorful pork in a rich, savory sauce with a balance of salty, tangy, and slightly sweet notes. It’s often enjoyed with steamed white rice.

Ingredients

  • 1kg pork belly or pork shoukder, cut into cubes
  • 1/2 soy sauce
  • 1/2 vinegar
  • 6 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp whole black peppercorns
  • 1-1 1/2 cups water (adjust based on preferred sauce consistency)
  • 1 tbsp sugar (optional)
  • 1-2 tbsp cooking oil

Steps

  1. Marinate
    In a bowl, combine pork, soy sauce, and half of the garlic. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes to 1 hour.
  2. Saute
    Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Sauté the remaining garlic until lightly browned.
  3. Add pork:
    Add the marinated pork to the pan and cook until lightly browned.
  4. Simmer:
    Add vinegar, bay leaves, peppercorns, and water. Do not stir after adding vinegar initially. Let it simmer uncovered for about 5 minutes.
  5. Cook Until Tender
    Cover and simmer on low heat for 40–50 minutes or until the pork is tender and the sauce has reduced to your liking. Stir occasionally.
  6. Final adjustments
    Add sugar if desired for a slightly sweet taste. Adjust soy sauce or vinegar to balance flavor according to preference.
  7. Serve
    Serve hot with steamed rice
