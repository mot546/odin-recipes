Chicken Adobo

Description

Chicken Adobo is a Filipino dish known for its tender chicken simmered in a savory and tangy sauce made from soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns. It’s widely considered a comfort food in the Philippines because of its rich flavor, easy preparation, and compatibility with rice.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken (leg quarters, drumsticks, or thighs), cut into serving pieces
  • 1/2 soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 6 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp whole black peppercorns
  • 1-1 1/2 cups water(optional)
  • 1 tbsp sugar(optiona, for slight sweetness)
  • 1-2 tbsp cooking oil

Steps

  1. Marinate Chicken
    Combine chicken, soy sauce, and half of the garlic in a bowl. Marinate for 30 minutes to 1 hour.
  2. Saute Garlic
    In a large pan, heat oil over medium heat. Sauté the remaining garlic until golden.
  3. Brown the Chicken
    Add the marinated chicken pieces and cook until slightly browned on all sides.
  4. Add Vinegar and Spices Pour in vinegar, bay leaves, peppercorns, and water (if you want more sauce). Let it simmer uncovered for a few minutes before stirring to let the vinegar cook off its sharpness.
  5. Simmer
    Cover the pan and simmer on low heat for 30–40 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is tender and the sauce is reduced to your liking.
  6. Adjust Taste
    Add sugar if desired. Adjust soy sauce or vinegar for your preferred balance of salty and tangy.
  7. Serve
    Serve hot with steamed rice.
