Sinampalukang Manok

Description

Sinampalukang Manok is a Filipino sour soup made with chicken, young tamarind leaves, and tamarind broth. It’s similar to sinigang but usually uses sautéed ginger and garlic to build a deeper flavor base. The dish is comforting, tangy, and commonly served with steamed rice.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken(preferably bone-in pieces)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger, sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped (optional)
  • 1 cup fresh tamarind leaves or sinigang mix(alternative)
  • 6 cups water
  • 1 long green chili
  • sault and fish sauce
  • cooking oil

Steps

  1. Saute Acromatics:
    In a pot, heat a bit of oil. Sauté garlic, onion, and ginger until fragrant and the onion is translucent.
  2. Add Chicken:
    Add chicken pieces and cook until lightly browned.
  3. Add Water:
    Pour in water and bring to a boil. Skim off any scum that rises to the top.
  4. Add Tamarind leaves or Mix:
    Add tamarind leaves or dissolve sinampalukan mix/tamarind powder into the broth. Stir well.
  5. Simmer:
    Lower heat and simmer for 30–40 minutes until the chicken is cooked through and tender.
  6. Season:
    Add salt or fish sauce to taste. Add long green chili if you prefer a slightly spicy broth.
  7. Serve:
    Serve hot with rice
